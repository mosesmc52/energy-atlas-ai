About Energy Atlas

Energy Atlas is an energy intelligence platform that transforms complex energy data into clear signals, alerts, and forward-looking insights.

It is designed for analysts, traders, and researchers who want to understand energy markets without manually stitching together data from multiple sources.

The Problem

Energy data is widely available, but difficult to use.

Answering basic questions like:

  • Is the natural gas market tightening?
  • Is demand increasing due to weather?
  • What changed this week?

often requires:

  • pulling data from multiple sources
  • building custom calculations
  • interpreting reports manually

This process is slow, repetitive, and difficult to scale.

What Energy Atlas Does

Energy Atlas simplifies this workflow by turning raw data into structured insight.

Instead of exploring datasets, users can:

  • view signals that describe current market conditions
  • receive alerts when important thresholds are reached
  • understand what is likely to happen next through simple forecasts
  • get context explaining why changes are occurring

How It Works

Energy Atlas organizes information into four layers:

Signals

Structured indicators that describe what is happening in the market.

Alerts

Automated checks that notify users when specific conditions are met.

Forecasts

Short-term projections based on recent trends and seasonal patterns.

Context

Summaries from energy reports that explain the drivers behind changes.

Focus

Energy Atlas currently focuses on natural gas markets, integrating:

  • storage
  • production
  • LNG exports
  • electricity demand
  • weather

This focus allows the platform to deliver deeper, more reliable insights instead of broad but shallow coverage.

Design Philosophy

Energy Atlas is built around a few key principles:

  • prioritize clarity over complexity
  • focus on signals instead of raw data
  • provide explainable outputs
  • deliver timely and actionable insight

Vision

The goal of Energy Atlas is to become the fastest way to understand what is happening in energy markets and what is likely to happen next.
